Proof David Olusoga Ogbolu

David Olusoga Ogbolu graduated as a Biomedical Scientist in 1997 with bias in Medical Microbiology. He was appointed as a Medical Laboratory Scientist at University College Hospital (UCH) after graduating. While working, he became a Fellow of Medical Laboratory Science Council, Nigeria and completed a Master of Science degree. He resigned his appointment at UCH in December 2006 and joined Ladoke Akintola University of Technology, Nigeria as a Lecturer II. In 2008, he received a British Society for Antimicrobial Chemotherapy research award to visit the Antimicrobials Research Group (ARG) at the University of Birmingham, UK where he was able to complete genomic research which contributed to the completion of his PhD degree in Medical Microbiology in Nigeria in 2010. In 2011 David was awarded a Royal Society Newton International Fellowship to continue his research, again in collaboration with ARG in the UK to investigate the genetic basis for carbapenem resistance in Nigerian pathogenic bacteria. He has since won several awards and grants for research, conferences, workshops, etc in different parts of the world. He rose through the rank to becoming a full Professor of Infectious Diseases and Antimicrobials in 2018 at Ladoke Akintola University of Technology. He has several published articles to his credit with immense contribution to knowledge in his field. He has been one of the few researchers able to investigate mechanisms of antibiotic resistance amongst West African isolates.
